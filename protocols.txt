## 1. Foundations of Health
**Protocol:** Foundations of Health Protocol

**PureGenomics UltraMultivitamin** `Supplement`
> Comprehensive multivitamin with activated B vitamins (methylfolate, methylcobalamin), chelated minerals, and antioxidants including CoQ10 and alpha-lipoic acid. A 2025 rapid review of 19 meta-analyses found benefits for cognition, blood pressure, and infection risk. Take 3 capsules daily with food.

**Magnesium Glycinate** `Supplement`
> Highly bioavailable chelated magnesium that is gentle on the stomach. Supports muscle relaxation, sleep quality, cardiovascular rhythm, and nervous system function. A 2024 umbrella meta-analysis showed magnesium supplementation significantly reduces blood pressure (SBP -1.25 mmHg, DBP -1.40 mmHg). Up to 50% of Americans are magnesium deficient. The glycinate form is particularly calming and ideal for evening use. Take 3 capsules before bed.

**PureLean Fiber** `Supplement`
> Comprehensive fiber blend including soluble and insoluble fibers (cellulose, partially hydrolyzed guar gum, flax seed, apple pectin, glucomannan). Highly suggestive evidence (Class II) supports reduced all-cause mortality. 95% of Americans don't meet fiber recommendations (~25-38g/day). Supports gut health, blood sugar balance, and satiety. Take 2 scoops 2-3 times daily with meals.

> The most studied ergogenic supplement, creatine supports ATP regeneration for muscle strength and power. A 2024 meta-analysis showed creatine plus resistance training significantly increases upper and lower body strength. Supports muscle maintenance, brain bioenergetics, and healthy aging.

### Vitamin D

```markdown
## 3. Vitamin D
**Protocol:** Vitamin D Optimization Protocol

### Primary Recommendation
**Vitamin D3 (1,000-2,000 IU daily)** `Supplement`
> D3 (cholecalciferol) is the naturally preferred and most bioactive form of vitamin D. A 2024 consensus statement supports daily vitamin D supplementation of 1,000-2,000 IU to raise serum 25(OH)D to optimal levels. The Endocrine Society and most American guidelines recommend maintaining levels of 30-50 ng/mL (75-125 nmol/L). Dosing should be adjusted based on baseline levels: severely deficient (<20 ng/mL) may need 4,000-5,000 IU daily for 8-12 weeks, then maintenance.

### Secondary Recommendations
**Superpower Blood Panel with Vitamin D Retest** `Testing` *(Check: service_request_history)*
> Confirms repletion to target levels (30-50 ng/mL per Endocrine Society guidelines) and allows dose adjustment for maintenance. Retest after 8-12 weeks of supplementation to verify adequacy. Monitor calcium levels in patients with granulomatous disease or primary hyperparathyroidism.

**Sun Exposure Protocol** `Lifestyle`
> UVB rays trigger natural vitamin D synthesis in skin. 10-20 minutes of midday sun (10am-2pm) on arms and legs can produce meaningful vitamin D, depending on latitude, season, and skin tone. Darker skin and northern latitudes require longer exposure. This complements but rarely replaces supplementation for those with documented deficiency. Always avoid sunburn.

**Vitamin D-Rich Foods** `Lifestyle`
> Dietary sources can contribute 600-1,000 IU daily when prioritized. Fatty fish are the richest natural sources: wild salmon (600-1,000 IU/3oz), mackerel (400 IU/3oz), sardines (250 IU/3oz), and cod liver oil (1,360 IU/tbsp). Egg yolks provide 40 IU each (pasture-raised contain 3-4x more). Fortified foods (milk, orange juice, cereals) typically add 100 IU per serving. While diet alone rarely corrects deficiency, regular consumption of fatty fish 2-3x weekly supports maintenance after repletion and provides synergistic omega-3 benefits.

### Evidence Sources
- [2024 Consensus Statement on Vitamin D](https://academic.oup.com/edrv/article/45/5/625/7659127) - Endocrine Reviews
- [Vitamin D 2000 IU Evidence Review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10857599/)
- Endocrine Society Clinical Practice Guidelines

### Safety Considerations
- Monitor calcium in granulomatous disease (sarcoidosis) or primary hyperparathyroidism
- Toxicity rare below 10,000 IU/day in healthy adults
- High-dose supplementation requires periodic monitoring
```
